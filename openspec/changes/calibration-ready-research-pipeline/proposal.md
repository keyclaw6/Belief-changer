## Why

The calibration factory cannot run without violating its own contracts: research has no executable lead/worker handoff, planner prompts admit forbidden reference material, and the production spec still assigns Opus outside the chapter-writer role. These gaps must be closed before run-001 so its baseline measures the factory rather than bootstrap ambiguity.

## What Changes

- Add blind, model-led multi-agent research in which a top-reasoning lead chooses its own communities, personas, subagents, searches, recursion, and sufficiency judgment while preserving visible evidence and provenance.
- Define comparable H-010/H-011 research arms and reconstructable quality/yield records without coupling the artifact contract to an orchestration framework or optimizing for cost, tokens, or speed.
- Make raw-source capture, research-log entries, worker yield, and the two synthesized research banks one traceable handoff.
- Remove reference-analysis inputs from research and planning, and make the brief an explicit framing input.
- **BREAKING:** reserve Opus 4.6 for the run-001 chapter-writing baseline; planning, review, research, and judging use the founder-approved non-writer model sets at top reasoning. Permit a later isolated Muse Spark 1.1 Stage-A writer arm without replacing the baseline.
- Clarify that calibration commits complete runs on `calibration-lab`, while normal production retains accepted-chapter commits to `main`.

### New Capabilities

- `deep-research`: Lead/worker decomposition, source independence, provenance-preserving yield packets, slot sufficiency, synthesis, and arm measurement.

### Modified Capabilities

- `book-pipeline`: Research handoffs, brief/framing responsibility, exact planner inputs, reviewer role constraints, and calibration commit behavior.
