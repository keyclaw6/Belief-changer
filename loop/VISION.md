# Current-Stage Vision: Factory-Level Recovery

**Status: FOUNDER-LOCKED.** This file is the controlling vision for the current factory-calibration stage. Every operator, planner, writer, reviewer, judge, and harness agent must read it before acting and must hold its work against it. No agent may edit, delete, rename, supersede, reinterpret away, or weaken this file. Only an explicit founder instruction may change it.

This stage vision does not replace `docs/VISION.md`. It translates that mission into the immediate problem the factory must solve.

## North star

Build a factory that reliably produces original belief-change books with the same method, narrative force, reading experience, and belief-changing movement as Allen Carr's books. The target is not a collection of Carr-like phrases. The target is a book that performs the same kind of live intervention on the reader: it recognizes their experience, exposes the trap, changes the perceived value of the behavior, removes shame and fear, and makes freedom feel like the obvious happy outcome.

Reference books are the standard for method and reading experience, never a source to copy. Method integrity, originality, evidence honesty, source traceability, non-shaming treatment, willpower-free logic, and medical safety remain non-negotiable.

## The failure we are correcting

After seven scored iterations, the best composite is 0.6675 against a 0.85 target. The loop produced one meaningful early lift and then plateaued. Recent iterations repeatedly translated broad judge findings into narrow wording rules—remove a caveat, ban a register, close a sentence-level fork—without establishing that wording was the root cause.

That is a factory-design failure. A weak chapter may originate in research, synthesis, framing, plan architecture, model assignment, context handoff, chapter sequencing, revision strategy, reviewer behavior, judge behavior, or the incentives created by scoring and gating. The system must diagnose the level of failure before prescribing a change.

## What the next review must accomplish

The next review is a whole-factory diagnosis, not another chapter score and not a prompt-editing exercise. It must:

1. Compare the current generated chapters directly with their position-matched reference chapters and explain the difference in reader experience, belief movement, narrative architecture, emotional progression, causal explanation, rhythm, and authority.
2. Review all completed iterations together: what changed, what scores moved, what gates accepted or rejected, what repeatedly failed, and which interventions treated symptoms rather than causes.
3. Trace the observed product defects backward through the entire production chain: research and synthesis; framing; master-plan construction and review; model choice; writer inputs and previous-chapter handoff; first-pass and possible second-pass writing; reviewer instructions and stopping rules; judge comparison; scoring, gating, and amendment selection.
4. Distinguish root causes from proxies. “Emotional authority is low” is a diagnosis of the reading experience, not automatically an instruction to add commands, certainty words, or capitals.
5. Consider changes at the level where the evidence points. Larger harness changes are allowed for consideration, including different planning structures, research requirements, chapter handoffs, context policies, multi-pass writing, a second model or editorial pass, reviewer design, judge synthesis, and the iteration strategy itself.
6. Produce a small, ranked set of high-leverage redesign options. Each option must state the evidence, causal mechanism, affected factory components, expected product change, risks, and the cheapest decisive test.
7. Say what should remain unchanged. The review must protect working parts of the factory and the method-integrity constraints instead of redesigning everything indiscriminately.

## Standard for a smart recommendation

A recommendation is strong only when it connects the artifact evidence to a factory cause and then to a testable system change:

**observed reader-level difference → responsible pipeline decision → proposed change → predicted artifact difference → decisive evaluation**

Lists of stylistic nits, generic writing advice, isolated prompt wording, and score chasing do not satisfy this standard. A prompt change may still be correct, but only when the review demonstrates that the prompt is the highest-leverage cause rather than the nearest editable file.

The reviewer must step back far enough to ask whether the factory is producing enacted discovery or merely describing a planned persuasion; whether authority is earned through diagnostic accuracy and demonstrated mechanism or simulated through tone; and whether chapter-to-chapter emotional movement is designed or left to emerge accidentally.

## Current-stage gate

No further iteration, prompt experiment, plan regeneration, chapter generation, or harness redesign begins until:

1. the founder says **go**;
2. the thorough whole-factory review has been created and delivered;
3. the founder chooses the redesign direction.

The new reviewer prompt must not be created before the founder says **go**. Until then, the complete current state is a checkpoint for diagnosis, not an accepted final factory design.

## Definition of success for this stage

This stage succeeds when the founder can see a clear, evidence-backed explanation for why the present factory produces careful belief-change curriculum instead of an Allen-Carr-like live intervention, and can choose a redesign that acts on the responsible system causes rather than another insignificant detail.
