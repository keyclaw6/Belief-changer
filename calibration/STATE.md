## Universal goal and loop

Quit-sugar and *Good Sugar Bad Sugar* are calibration specimens only; the reference remains evaluator-only, and untouched holdout performance is the proof against overfit.

Use the simplest empirical loop: freeze one generic hypothesis and its inputs; produce the complete Chapters 1–3 first-draft batch before reviewing or revising any chapter; measure it mechanically, internally, and with independent blind judges covering distinct dimensions; record verdict, causal rivals, and autopsy; then keep, revert, or replace the one lever. Metrics and internal reviews can block promotion but cannot suppress target-panel diagnosis when three readable artifacts exist. Repeat to Chapter parity, full-book parity and surpass, then zero-tuning holdout generalization. Never tune one chapter in isolation.
