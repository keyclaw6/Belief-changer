# Grounded audit — C-02 R1

**Verdict: MATERIAL DEFECT**

## Material findings

1. **Unassigned physiological claim**

   Candidate passage:

   > “Does a bowl of rice convert to glucose in your body? Naturally — that is part of what it's for.”

   Controlling boundary:

   > “EV-17 is distinction-supporting evidence only. It does not scientifically derive the GOOD SUGAR/BAD SUGAR method boundary.”

   > “Its narrow contribution here is that source, dose, and exposure context matter.”

   **Defect category:** Unassigned evidence import / physiological mechanism.

   Neither assigned EV-17 packet supplies a claim about rice digestion or the teleological assertion that glucose conversion is “what it's for.” Using this biochemical statement while defining protected food gives the operational boundary unsupported physiological authority.

2. **Incomplete immediate metabolic-evidence firewall**

   Candidate passage:

   > “I am not asking you to use this finding to be frightened. I am asking you to notice that it confirms the value of a clear target: ordinary nourishment is not the target, and the science does not make it so.”

   Controlling boundary:

   > “Any metabolic fact used must be immediately detached from diagnosis, treatment advice, guaranteed benefit, or fear as motivation.”

   **Defect category:** Critical evidence and clinical-safety firewall.

   The passage disowns fear but does not immediately detach the hepatic-insulin-resistance material from self-diagnosis, treatment decisions, or guaranteed benefit. The later general safety notice does not satisfy the binding immediacy requirement.

3. **Unsupported mechanism and circumstances added to the S-010 caregiver account**

   Candidate passage:

   > “She didn't need willpower. She didn't need a complex plan. She looked at the product, saw something she hadn't noticed before, and made a different choice.”

   Controlling boundary:

   > “It supports only that information can affect reported household choices.”

   > “After WIC nutritionist advice, this caregiver reported changing a child’s drinks toward water and checking sugar labels.”

   **Defect category:** Attribution, circumstance, and mechanism invention.

   The source reports actions after advice. It does not establish absence of willpower, absence of a complex plan, or a previously unnoticed discovery. Attaching those method-favorable details to a published participant materially converts a bounded report into unsupported testimony about how the change occurred.

4. **Unsupported personal and product circumstances added to the S-011 shopper account**

   Candidate passages:

   > “The distrust was real. The care was real. The homemade drink was made with genuine attention.”

   > “The homemade label — the love, the local tradition, the real ingredients — didn't change what was in the glass.”

   Controlling boundary:

   > “One shopper explained avoiding commercial sweet beverages through distrust of their ingredients. The full response reports homemade sobolo substitution…”

   > “The permitted use is that homemade or reassuringly presented products still require the same categorical definition…”

   **Defect category:** Participant attribution and circumstance invention.

   Distrust and homemade sobolo are supported. The participant’s “care,” “genuine attention,” “love,” and the characterization “real ingredients” are not. The commission permits care to be protected as an illustrative value; it does not permit those qualities to be presented as facts about this research participant.

5. **Behavioral absence is incorrectly made sufficient for desire-level closure**

   Candidate passage:

   > “The destination of no planned doses is not a punishment. It is the only position from which the debate genuinely ends. Where there are no planned exceptions, there is nothing to anticipate, nothing to bargain over, nothing to recover from.”

   Controlling boundaries:

   > “Behavioral absence and continuing reward fantasy can coexist.”

   > “This promises the intended operating freedom; it does not claim that the reader has already crossed the later chosen threshold or guarantee an outcome or timeline.”

   **Defect category:** Outcome, generality, and critical method-integrity overclaim.

   The commission authorizes no planned doses as the destination and says recurring planned exceptions are incompatible with closure. It does not authorize the converse claim that merely having no planned exceptions guarantees an end to anticipation or bargaining. EV-07 expressly preserves the distinction between behavioral absence and changed valuation. The passage collapses that distinction and prematurely guarantees the later freedom state.

6. **The mandatory household safety perimeter is absent**

   Candidate household passages include:

   > “A banana in your child's lunchbox.”

   > “The first account shows that information about what's in a product can genuinely change behavior — that looking honestly at the label is worth doing.”

   Controlling boundaries:

   > “The chapter must carry its assigned safety notice.”

   > “Household material concerns an adult examining their own decision loop. It may model calm, non-moral definitions, but it may not prescribe a child’s diet, create guilt about a child’s body, infer child-health outcomes, or override pediatric or other qualified advice.”

   **Defect category:** Critical household-safety omission.

   The chapter uses child-food examples and caregiver evidence, but its safety notice never states that the method concerns the adult reader’s own loop or that pediatric and other qualified advice outrank it for children. SAFE-03 is an expressly owned, mandatory boundary here, not an optional detail.
