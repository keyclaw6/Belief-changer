# Grounded audit — C-01 R1

**Verdict: MATERIAL DEFECT**

## Material findings

1. **Candidate passage:** “BAD SUGAR were a difficult employee you can never quite fire” and “BAD SUGAR — the specific substance this book targets.”

   **Controlling boundary:** “BAD SUGAR may be used as the book’s target label, but its operational boundary belongs to C-02; do not improvise a definition,” and DEF-02 defines it as “A method label with a categorical core and one behavior-resolved edge.”

   **Category:** Target-definition alteration and chapter-ownership breach.

   Calling BAD SUGAR a “substance” prematurely recasts a behavioral product category as a molecular entity. That can materially spread the target toward sugar as an ingredient, contrary to the protected C-02 boundary.

2. **Candidate passage:** “Nell Kauls is a woman…”

   **Controlling boundary:** S-021 authorizes the identity “Nell Kauls, named interviewee” and identifies her as “a Nutritional Weight & Wellness client and educator.”

   **Category:** Demographic invention.

   The accepted packet does not state the demographic identity “woman.” Adding it exceeds the retained identity scope and could misidentify the named source.

3. **Candidate passage:** “Nell Kauls is a woman who described her experience with food and sugar in a 2024 interview on the *Dishing Up Nutrition* podcast.”

   **Controlling boundary:** S-021 requires: “Nell Kauls, interviewed by Brandy Buro; Dishing Up Nutrition, Nutritional Weight & Wellness; 4 April 2024.” The commission additionally identifies it as “a public commercial-wellness transcript.”

   **Category:** Required-attribution and publication-circumstance breach.

   The attribution omits the interviewer, publisher, exact date, and commercial-wellness context. Those are binding source circumstances, particularly important for preventing a commercial testimonial from appearing as independent outcome evidence.

4. **Candidate passage:** “If your life with sugar has moved from open warfare to a stable ceasefire, you have achieved something most people never do.”

   **Controlling boundary:** “Do not invent an author-origin story, clinical pedigree, success rate, scale-of-success claim…” EV-06 is scoped to “One commercial wellness testimonial.”

   **Category:** Unsupported prevalence and scale claim.

   Neither assigned packet establishes how many people achieve a managed truce. “Most people never do” invents population-scale rarity from no licensed evidence.

5. **Candidate passage:** “The quiet knowledge that if you stopped managing, the old pattern would likely return — because the thing you're managing against hasn't actually changed.”

   **Controlling boundary:** EV-06 permits only that “Genuine relief can coexist with substantial continuing management” in one testimonial. The commission says not to infer “that management is inferior for every reader.”

   **Category:** Unsupported probability, mechanism, and generality.

   Kauls reported daily management; she did not report the counterfactual outcome of stopping it. The chapter invents both a likely return and an unchanged underlying mechanism, then assigns that prediction to the reader generally.

6. **Candidate passage:** “Ryan Andrews is a registered dietitian who wrote publicly about his own relationship with dessert.”

   **Controlling boundary:** S-023 requires: “Ryan Andrews, MS, MA, RD, RYT, CSCS; Precision Nutrition.”

   **Category:** Required-attribution and publication-context breach.

   The passage supplies only one credential and omits the required publisher. Although it correctly identifies a personal account, it does not preserve the packet’s binding attribution.

7. **Candidate passages:** “Not *resisting* desserts. Not *managing* desserts. Not missing them.” And: “There is a difference between not eating BAD SUGAR and not wanting it.”

   **Controlling boundary:** “The permitted inference is only that one person reported a distinction between not eating dessert and no longer missing it, and that decision burden can diminish.” His account “must not be presented as proof of this book’s exact category.”

   **Category:** Outcome inflation and target substitution.

   The source supports “not missing desserts” and diminished decision burden. It does not establish an absolute absence of resistance or management, nor “not wanting” the book’s broader BAD SUGAR category. The passage strengthens the outcome and substitutes the book’s target for Andrews’s personal target.

8. **Candidate passage:** “Most approaches to sugar operate on the same assumption…”

   **Controlling boundary:** “Packet material outside EV-06 and EV-08 is not licensed for this chapter.” The C-01 evidence assignment is exclusively EV-06 and EV-08.

   **Category:** Unsupported method-prevalence generalization.

   Neither packet surveys sugar approaches or their assumptions. The assertion that “most” share one model is unassigned authority material.

9. **Candidate passage:** “something becomes possible that no diet can offer: freedom without deprivation, without battle, and without a lifetime of rules.”

   **Controlling boundary:** M-02 “names the intended standard for freedom” but “does not promise an effortless timetable.” The commission also prohibits inferring general efficacy or that management is inferior for every reader.

   **Category:** Unsupported comparative efficacy and generality.

   The frozen freedom standard is authorized; the assertion that no diet can offer it is not. It makes an evidence-free universal superiority claim over all diets and managed approaches.

10. **Candidate passage:** “Because every plan you've tried before asked you to change the behavior first and sort out the wanting later.”

    **Controlling boundary:** The commission supplies no history of every reader’s prior plan and licenses only EV-06 and EV-08.

    **Category:** Invented reader circumstances and universal method claim.

    The target-reader description supports exhaustion with prior attempts, not the assertion that every prior plan used this sequence. The invented history is then used as evidence for the chapter’s causal explanation.

11. **Candidate passages:** “The want stays. The walls get more sophisticated.” And: “That's backwards. It puts the wall up before you've examined whether the thing behind it deserves to be fenced in. No wonder the walls keep needing repair.”

    **Controlling boundary:** C-01 “introduces that destination…without explaining or proving the later benefit-demolition argument” and “must leave the full explanation of failed control methods…to later chapters.” C-05 owns the “Anti-method chapter.”

    **Category:** Reserved-chapter ownership.

    These passages do more than distinguish managed control from decision quiet: they give the causal explanation that behavior-first methods preserve wanting and therefore require continued repair. That materially performs C-05’s reserved anti-method argument.

12. **Candidate passage:** “If the examination does its job… the behavior change will follow naturally, easily, as the obvious thing to do. Not the hard thing.”

    **Controlling boundary:** EV-08 prohibits “causation, probability, general efficacy, or universal ease.” M-02 “does not promise an effortless timetable,” and C-01 may not assure readers that they will reproduce Andrews’s experience.

    **Category:** Efficacy, causation, and ease promise.

    This guarantees a natural and easy behavioral outcome conditional on the book’s examination. No assigned evidence supports that causal progression or its ease, and it can turn later difficulty into an implied failure of understanding.

13. **Candidate passage:** “So eat your lunch. Have your usual afternoon snack. If you were going to have dessert tonight, have it. All I ask is that you…notice, as you eat, whether the experience actually matches what you anticipated.”

    **Controlling boundary:** “I-01 is the chapter’s sole instruction” and “must not be supplemented with…a ceremonial final dose, compensation, or any other behavioral assignment.”

    **Category:** Instruction-spine and critical method-integrity breach.

    The passage adds explicit consumption directions and a food-experience observation exercise. Those are behavioral assignments beyond I-01, including prescribed dessert consumption, and prematurely begin later perception work during the protected reading period.

## Authority-boundary confirmation

Checked the exclusive C-01 assignments EV-06/S-021 and EV-08/S-023; exact-quotation status; M-01, M-02, M-06, and I-01; the C-02 target-definition handoff; later anti-method ownership; agency, non-shaming and willpower-free method; clinical priority; eating-disorder safety; and the claims perimeter. The two approved quoted excerpts remained textually exact, but their surrounding attribution and inference boundaries were crossed as listed above.

No identifiable unassigned source was imported. Several unsupported prevalence, method, probability, and efficacy assertions were invented without any assigned packet basis.
