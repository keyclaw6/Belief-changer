# AUTHORITATIVE SEMANTIC COMMISSION — C-02

C-02, “Draw the Line, Free the Rest,” owns one belief move: a precise behavioral target makes practical totality compatible with ordinary eating, intact agency, and a non-catastrophic margin for accidents and uncertainty. The approximately 3,000-word chapter must resolve the fear that “fruit is sugar too,” hidden ingredients make quitting impossible, or one boundary will spread into fear of all food. Its emotional movement is light demolition and an early glimpse of freedom, not a demand that the reader stop now.

The central distinction is behavioral and operational, not a claim that foods fall into morally or physiologically uniform classes:

- **GOOD SUGAR (DEF-01)** is the method label for sugars and starches found in ordinary nourishment: whole fruit, vegetables, legumes, nuts, plain dairy, and ordinary staples such as oats, rice, potatoes, bread, and pasta. “Good” is neither moral approval nor a biochemical verdict. These foods are outside the target.

- **BAD SUGAR (DEF-02)** has a categorical core: products conventionally consumed as sugar-sweetened drinks, confection, dessert, sweet baked goods, or added-sugar sweet snacks, including products sweetened with sugar, honey, or syrup. Soda, sugar-sweetened coffee or tea, sweets, biscuits, cakes, pastries, ice cream, dessert products, and prominently sweetened cereals or bars are core examples. Hunger, homemade status, care, celebration, convenience, or a natural, organic, dark, low-sugar, or healthier label cannot exempt a core item.

- Only a genuinely ambiguous refined savoury snack has a behavior-resolved edge. It enters BAD SUGAR when it is functioning as a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or automatic nibble rather than ordinary meal food. This role inquiry may bring an ambiguous savoury item inside the line but may never move a core sweet item outside it.

- Small incidental ingredients do not turn an ordinary meal into BAD SUGAR. Items inside BAD SUGAR are not asserted to have identical physiology.

- Under **DEF-03**, “sugar,” “a sugar hit,” and “quitting sugar” mean BAD SUGAR as defined here—not fruit, ordinary meals, every carbohydrate, or ingredient-level purity. “Hit” and “planned dose” describe an intentional decision unit, not drug equivalence.

The destination inside this line is no planned BAD SUGAR doses (**DEF-04**). Moderation remains a legitimate legal, moral, and practical choice. It is simply not presented as desire-level closure when recurring planned exceptions preserve the proposition that selected moments need a sugar upgrade. State this distinction without conducting the later full demolition of weekends, holidays, tapering, stashes, or other exception strategies. The reader’s right to choose remains intact; the book makes closure available but does not claim authority over the choice.

The **Molecule Margin (DEF-05; LX-P04)** covers a trace ingredient, uncertain restaurant sauce, accidental bite, or genuine classification mistake. None is a deliberate return, purity failure, or command to continue. The reader may notice it, learn from it if useful, and continue from the choice already made. The margin does not disguise a planned exception as an accident and must never become an ingredient-surveillance practice.

Whole fruit and ordinary meal carbohydrates are expressly protected from category creep. One hundred percent juice, dried fruit, non-nutritive sweeteners, alcohol, caffeine, and adjacent indulgences are not required quits and do not define success (**DEF-06**). A reader may examine any of them separately later, but this chapter may neither expand the present target to include them nor prescribe them as a consolation or replacement regime.

Debut **LX-P01, the Ordinary Eating Compass**, as temporary teaching authority that restores ordinary nourishment and real-life pleasure to their proper status. It is not a food-policing system or permanent dashboard and succeeds by becoming unnecessary. Its only boundary instrument here is **LX-P02, the meal-or-hit check**: use it solely at the genuine refined-savoury gray edge to ask whether the item is serving ordinary eating or functioning as a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or automatic nibble. It cannot exempt any categorical-core item.

Use **PX-03** once: sort a few representative examples into categorical core, protected ordinary food, genuine gray savoury edge, and Molecule Margin. Its sole job is to teach the distinction and then stop. It must involve no repeated logging, scoring, label-scanning homework, dietary restriction, prescribed consumption, or compensation.

**AN-08, the label costume rack**, conveys that altered ingredients, homemade status, or reassuring marketing language do not move a core sweet product outside the definition. It does not imply that every formulation has identical effects. **AN-09, the customs desk for molecules**, exposes how ingredient perfection would create endless border checks and establishes the operational relief supplied by the Molecule Margin.

The assigned household material supports **SC-07**, whose job is to protect genuine care and useful knowledge while showing that a label or homemade presentation cannot redraw the target.
