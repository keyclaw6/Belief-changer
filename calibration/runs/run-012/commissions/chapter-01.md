# AUTHORITATIVE SEMANTIC COMMISSION — C-01

C-01, “The Debate Can End,” is a 2,800-word entry contract operating at D0/F1. Its single belief move is that reading this book does not enroll the reader in another diet, demand an immediate quitting decision, or require discipline to protect them from a prized pleasure. The reader may investigate while eating normally. The outcome under consideration is freedom from the recurring sugar debate, not increasingly perfect control of it.

Receive a skeptical adult exhausted by diets, moderation rules, private bargaining, lapses, and restarts. Address R-05 first: the reader whose boundaries and resets have brought real relief but still require continuing maintenance. Address R-06 alongside them: the reader who wants the option to stop demanding decisions rather than another perfect streak. RV-09 supplies recognition of a workable plan that never finishes its shift; RV-11 supplies the wish for the debate itself to end. These are plan-authored reader dialect, not participant quotations.

The chapter owns three outcome distinctions:

- Suppression or perfect control can leave the anticipated reward and the need for discipline intact.
- A managed truce can provide genuine relief while retaining daily actions, boundaries, exceptions, and resets. It is a legitimate reader-owned outcome and must not be mocked or called fraudulent.
- The book’s desired freedom is no planned BAD SUGAR doses and no continuing debate because BAD SUGAR is no longer an active reward, rescue, or special-occasion proposition. This is practical decision quiet, not ingredient purity, universal carbohydrate restriction, dietary perfection, or a promised health result.

This chapter introduces that destination without asking the reader to choose it yet and without explaining or proving the later benefit-demolition argument. BAD SUGAR may be used as the book’s target label, but its operational boundary belongs to C-02; do not improvise a definition or let the target spread to fruit, ordinary meals, every carbohydrate, or trace ingredients.

Debut these frozen formulations exactly:

- M-01: `You can test whether BAD SUGAR owns any special or irreplaceable pleasure, without denying pleasant taste or giving up your right to choose.` This is the autonomy and inquiry contract, not an efficacy guarantee.
- M-02: `without deprivation, without battle, and without a lifetime of rules` This names the intended standard for freedom and distinguishes it from suppression and permanent management. It does not promise an effortless timetable.
- M-06: `anticipating, bargaining, and starting over` This identifies the recurring decision labor the reader wants to leave behind; it is not a physiological claim.

Do not debut later mantra language or the book’s later mechanism vocabulary.

I-01 is the chapter’s sole instruction and must appear verbatim:

`READ WITH CURIOSITY, NOT DREAD; UNTIL YOU REACH YOUR CHOSEN THRESHOLD, EAT AS YOU NORMALLY DO, BEGIN NO NEW RESTRICTION OR COMPENSATION PLAN FOR THIS METHOD, AND KEEP FOLLOWING ANY QUALIFIED CLINICAL INSTRUCTION.`

Its time limit is essential. It governs only the reading period before the reader’s eventual chosen threshold. It is not a permanent eating direction, does not suspend medically required care, and must not be supplemented with reduction, tapering, tracking, cupboard-clearing, a ceremonial final dose, compensation, or any other behavioral assignment.

EV-06 provides the bounded managed-truce account. The source is S-021, a public commercial-wellness transcript: Nell Kauls, interviewed by Brandy Buro; *Dishing Up Nutrition*, Nutritional Weight & Wellness; 4 April 2024. Kauls described substantial relief while also reporting a long maintenance history, daily actions to keep sugar cravings at bay, firm personal boundaries, challenging special occasions, exceptions, and returns to her usual plan at the next meal or day. The permitted exact quotation is:

- `“I feel like I've been let out of jail.”` — S-021#E-001, transcript freedom moment, EXACT_QUOTE.

Treat this solely as Kauls’s metaphor for relief from obsessive food thoughts, not as a clinical finding or a promise to the reader. SC-08 may use the supported holiday or special-event pie context followed by a practiced return to plan to make continuing management visible. Preserve both sides without contradiction: her relief was genuine, and her account still involved ongoing labor. Do not infer commercial-program efficacy, verified abstinence, absence of desire, universal trigger categories, or that management is inferior for every reader.

EV-08 provides the bounded contrast. The source is S-023, Ryan Andrews, MS, MA, RD, RYT, CSCS; Precision Nutrition; one author’s published self-report, not a trial. Andrews reported that before the change he spent at least two hours each week debating whether to eat dessert. He later reported twelve months without desserts, sweets, candy, or added sugar across birthdays and major holidays, reduced dessert-decision time, and removing desserts from his active decision catalog. The materially useful exact language is:

- `“not missing desserts”` — short exact excerpt from S-023#E-001, Month 1.

SC-01 may render his recurring weekly dessert debate in original prose to make private decision labor concrete. The permitted inference is only that one person reported a distinction between not eating dessert and no longer missing it, and that decision burden can diminish. His target was his own, future desserts were not described as permanently forbidden, and his account must not be presented as proof of this book’s exact category, permanent totality, a moderation result, causation, probability, general efficacy, or universal ease. His reported physical or mental improvement and fruit tasting sweeter perform no C-01 job and must not be used as promises.

EV-06 and EV-08 are not matched cases and do not compare two methods. Their contrast is conceptual: substantial relief can coexist with continuing management, while diminished decision burden and not missing dessert are possible in one separate self-report. Do not imply that the two people followed the same target, used the same approach, or establish which outcome another reader will have. Packet material outside EV-06 and EV-08 is not licensed for this chapter.

Trust must come from exact recognition, a testable offer, and honest limits. State plainly in substance that the book offers an investigation rather than asking for belief on the strength of authority credentials. Do not invent an author-origin story, clinical pedigree, success rate, scale-of-success claim, testimonial composite, or assurance that the reader will reproduce Andrews’s experience.

Maintain agency throughout. The reader is neither ordered to stop nor diagnosed, shamed, moralized at, or treated as weak-willed. Pleasant taste, real emotion, ordinary hunger, and genuine social pleasure remain conceded. The chapter may reject a discipline test but must leave the full explanation of failed control methods and the substantive examination of sugar’s claimed benefits to later chapters. It must make no weight-loss, metabolic, energy, mental-clarity, craving-timeline, or taste-reset promise and must not present the method as eating-disorder or medical treatment.

Hand C-02 a willing investigator who has begun no new restriction, retains the right to choose, understands that I-01 expires at the chosen threshold, and can now receive a precise target definition without fearing that the inquiry has already become another diet.
