# AUTHORITATIVE SEMANTIC COMMISSION — CH-01

CH-01 owns one belief move: replace “success means controlling sugar for longer” with the more valuable possibility that sugar can cease to feel missing and cease to require debate. The chapter must distinguish three outcomes without presenting them as a universal progression: behavior held down while desire remains; genuine relief accompanied by continuing management; and the possible quiet in which the item leaves the active decision field. The reader’s previous struggle does not establish weak character, and a longer streak is not the book’s measure of freedom.

Address especially the restriction veteran, managed-truce maintainer, and decision-tax escapee (`PER-02`, `PER-05`, `PER-06`). Honour rules and boundaries that have brought real relief; do not belittle them merely because they still require work. At the same time, let the reader want something beyond a plan that demands daily upkeep: the end of `anticipating, bargaining, and starting over` (`M-06`, frozen wording). The chapter’s assigned image, `SA-01`, is the contrast between two outwardly similar absences and two different inner states. Not eating while preserving a future reward is not the same outcome as no longer missing it.

The destination introduced here is autonomy-led practical total freedom inside the precise target line that `CH-02` will define: no planned doses within that line, but no molecular-purity project, perpetual moderation dashboard, food fear, or identity organized around quitting. Ordinary eating, pleasant taste, company, celebrations, and qualified clinical care remain intact. The reader retains every legal and moral choice. Present no-planned-dose freedom as the book’s intended destination, not as a demand for immediate compliance or a guaranteed result. Do not stage a quit, teach a threshold, or imply that freedom must be earned through elapsed time.

The chapter must debut these promises verbatim:

`You can test whether BAD SUGAR owns any special or irreplaceable pleasure, without denying pleasant taste or giving up your right to choose.` (`M-02`)

`without deprivation, without battle, and without a lifetime of rules` (`M-03`)

The triad is both a destination promise and an outcome test for methods; it is not an empirical guarantee that every reader will reproduce a particular testimony. `BAD SUGAR` appears within the frozen promise, but this chapter must not improvise its definition. Its precise operational, non-purity meaning belongs to `CH-02`, which receives from this chapter the need for a livable target line.

The evidence establishes outcome contrasts only:

Ryan Andrews’s author-published first-person report for Precision Nutrition (`S-023`) supplies the clearest two-state comparison. Before the later experiment, he reported spending “at least 2 hours each week” debating dessert (`S-023#E-003`, permitted exact snippet, Month 1), while an earlier four-month dessert-free bodybuilding period retained nighttime dreams and a stash intended for after the diet (`S-023#E-004`, interpretation grounded in Lesson 9). Later, he reported twelve months without desserts, sweets, candy, or added sugar through birthdays and major holidays (`S-023#E-002`), said he found himself “not missing desserts” (`S-023#E-001`, exact quotation fragment, Month 1), and described removing desserts from his “decision catalog” without declaring them metaphysically forbidden (`S-023#E-006`, permitted exact source language, Lesson 9 and “Will I ever eat dessert again?”). This can illustrate the difference between not eating and no longer missing. It cannot establish this book’s efficacy, a mechanism, probability, universal ease, durable freedom for others, or a general moderation result. His scope does not cover every refined or junk carbohydrate. Exclude his reported physical, mental, and sensory changes as chapter payoffs.

The qualitative feasibility study by Madigan CD, Hill AJ, Caterson ID, Burk J, Hendy C, and Chalkley A, *Nutrients* 15(6), 1389, DOI 10.3390/nu15061389 (`S-020`), supplies a bounded suppression example. One participant described denial as displacement or worsening (`S-020#E-001`, her belief, not an established backfire mechanism) and called the first six weeks of an eight-week intervention “quite a battle” before reporting better choices in the final two weeks (`S-020#E-002`, permitted exact phrase and interpretation, Results §3.12, Female, 58 years). The study concerned indulgent foods and beverages, not added sugar alone. It offers neither a durable post-study outcome nor evidence that restriction universally produces obsession or withdrawal.

Nell Kauls’s interview with Brandy Buro for *Dishing Up Nutrition*, Nutritional Weight & Wellness, 4 April 2024 (`S-021`), supplies the managed-truce contrast. In this commercial wellness-testimonial setting, Kauls described relief from obsessive food thoughts with the exact metaphor “I feel like I’ve been let out of jail” (`S-021#E-001`, exact quotation, transcript freedom moment). She also described more than fifteen years of daily actions to keep sugar cravings at bay (`S-021#E-002`), boundaries around particular foods, extra management in vacations, restaurants, illness, alcohol, and special occasions, and returns to her usual plan at the next meal or day (`S-021#E-004`–`E-006`). Honour the relief as genuine while observing that the account still contains continuing management. Do not infer program efficacy, continuous sugar abstinence, universal maintenance needs, failure, or absence of desire. Her weight report is not available as a payoff.

These accounts are testimony about differing lived states, not a success scale and not proof of a common staircase. Do not merge their populations, targets, or methods. Do not invent dialogue, participant detail, causation, an author escape story, medical outcomes, or a mechanism explaining why the states differ. CH-01 establishes that the outcome distinction matters; later chapters own the explanation and belief-by-belief argument.

Authority under `STR-01` must come from transparent limits, respect for the reader’s experience, and the bounded contrast between control and decision quiet—not from pedigree, conversion narrative, testimonials presented as proof, prevalence claims, or promised replication. Admit pleasant taste and uncertainty. Do not use addiction, dopamine, withdrawal, fear, disgust, weight loss, mental clarity, bodily transformation, or food morality to strengthen the invitation. Do not prescribe calories, carbohydrates, meal timing, ingredient targets, or any other nutrition program.

The chapter’s instruction must appear verbatim:

`KEEP AN OPEN MIND: TEST EVERY CLAIM AGAINST YOUR OWN EXPERIENCE, AND KEEP QUALIFIED MEDICAL OR EATING-DISORDER CARE IN CHARGE OF EVERY CLINICAL DECISION.` (`INS-01`)

The book is not eating-disorder treatment, and prescribed glucose, medical nutrition therapy, metabolic or diabetes care, pregnancy-related advice, and other qualified clinical instructions outrank it. End the chapter’s semantic handoff with the unresolved need for a precise, non-purity target; `CH-02` owns that definition. The chapter budget is 2,800 words.
