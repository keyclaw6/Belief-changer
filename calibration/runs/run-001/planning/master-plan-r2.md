## Target behavior

The target is compulsive consumption and mental management of `BAD SUGAR`: the anticipation, reward, rescue, exception, grazing, bargaining, lapse, and reset loop around the defined category.

The target is not:

- Food or eating
- Fruit
- Every carbohydrate
- Ordinary meal staples
- Molecular purity
- Weight loss
- Diabetes or metabolic treatment
- Eating-disorder treatment
- Every pleasant sweet taste

## Primary reader and six functional personas

The book addresses one general adult through six functions:

1. **Sweet-Reward Reader** — taste, reward, dessert, sweet drinks, or quick lift appear uniquely valuable.
2. **Restriction Veteran** — denial produces battle, and one event becomes interpreted as total failure.
3. **Context Carrier** — emotion, social expectation, availability, convenience, and time pressure make sugar seem inevitable.
4. **Informed Household Translator** — labels and household choices matter, but knowledge risks becoming purity surveillance or caregiver moralizing.
5. **Managed-Truce Maintainer** — boundaries and resets have brought genuine relief but still require continuing labour.
6. **Decision-Tax Escapee** — the desired outcome is irrelevance rather than prohibition or a perfect streak.

The household persona incorporates P-04 and P-05. Caregiver evidence never becomes evidence of the caregiver’s own abstinence or freedom.

## Load-bearing false belief

**BAD SUGAR gives me a special pleasure, reward, comfort, or quick lift that ordinary food and ordinary life cannot provide, so removing it would leave me deprived.**

The book disputes uniqueness, ownership, necessity, and irreplaceability. It does not deny pleasant taste, calories, appetite, distress, celebration, or the reader’s reported experience.

## Redefinition decision

### GOOD SUGAR

Sugars and starches occurring in ordinary nourishment, including whole fruit, vegetables, legumes, nuts, plain dairy, oats, rice, potatoes, bread, pasta, and other ordinary meal staples.

### BAD SUGAR

Either:

1. A sugar-sweetened drink, confection, dessert, sweet baked good, or added-sugar sweet snack, whether sweetened with sugar, honey, or syrup; or
2. A genuinely ambiguous refined savoury snack functioning as a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or automatic nibble rather than ordinary meal food.

Behavioral-role inquiry applies only to the genuinely gray refined-savoury edge. It cannot exempt a core sweet drink, confection, dessert, sweet baked good, or sweet snack.

Small incidental ingredients in an ordinary meal do not transform the meal into BAD SUGAR.

### Definitional decree

**Throughout this book, whenever I say sugar, a sugar hit, or quitting sugar, take me to mean BAD SUGAR as defined here—not fruit, ordinary meals, or every carbohydrate.**

### Totality inside the line

The destination is no planned BAD SUGAR doses. This is practical total freedom, not molecular purity or forfeiture of autonomy. Moderation remains a legal and moral option, but recurring exceptions, bargaining, and resets are not renamed desire-level freedom.

### Margin for error

`the Molecule Margin` covers:

- Trace ingredients
- Uncertain restaurant sauces
- An accidental bite
- An honest classification mistake
- Incidental ingredients in an ordinary meal

The margin prevents panic and all-or-nothing collapse. It does not convert a planned exception into an accident.

### Conditional exclusions

One hundred percent juice, dried fruit, non-nutritive sweeteners, alcohol, caffeine, and adjacent indulgences are outside the required quit. Later examination of them is optional and does not alter the success criterion.

## Fork decisions

| Fork | Locked position |
|---|---|
| Fork 1 — personification | Externalize the sugar trap, learned sweet-reward script, deprivation method, and documented commercial optimization. Never create an inner creature, parasite, attacking voice, or neurological entity. |
| Fork 2 — autonomy and totality | Preserve the reader’s authority while making no planned BAD SUGAR doses the simpler destination. Never command abstinence or claim moderation is impossible. Expose the continuing symbolic value and decision cost of planned exceptions. |
| Fork 3 — science | Use light, claim-graded science in the prose. Place the complete evidence-and-limits battery in Chapter 19. Facts clarify distinctions and are immediately disowned as fear tactics. |
| Fork 4 — villain | Direct justified anger toward actor-specific historical formulation, testing, packaging, promotion, and marketing practices, and toward the deprivation method. Do not attribute intent to generic retailers, workplaces, cafés, celebrations, families, or clinicians. |

## Medical and eating-disorder perimeter

Medical glucose, prescribed nutrition, diabetes or metabolic treatment, pregnancy-related dietary advice, persistent weakness, and eating-disorder care sit outside the belief method.

Qualified clinical instructions outrank the book. The book supplies no medication changes, glucose targets, calorie targets, carbohydrate prescription, withdrawal schedule, or crisis treatment.

# 2. Book-level through-line

The reader arrives wanting the sugar debate to stop but expecting that stopping means losing a pleasure. The book first narrows the target so fruit, ordinary meals, medicine, and accidental molecules remain safe. It then preserves agency while showing that choices can be loaded by learned value: a rehearsed reward proposition activates anticipation, and a sugar hit may quiet that episode while receiving more credit than its taste earned. The benefit-demolition chapters return reward, comfort, convenience, belonging, and care to accomplishment, pause, ordinary food, people, and occasion. Documentary evidence then distinguishes deliberate historical optimization from ordinary availability, while the science chapters distinguish subjective difficulty from unsupported addiction, withdrawal, and compulsion claims. The book exposes deprivation, purity, powerless identity, planned exceptions, and managed truce as different ways of keeping BAD SUGAR important. After one bounded testimony and an explicit challenge to the method itself, the reader audits the installed understandings, chooses a threshold without waiting for a ceremonial date, and receives exact interpretations for thoughts, accidents, planned returns, and social exposure. The ending retires every teaching tool and leaves a quiet identity: ordinary life remains, and there is no sugar question left to settle.
