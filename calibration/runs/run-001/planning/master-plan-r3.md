## 1. Locked book decisions

### Format and length

### Target behavior

The target is compulsive consumption and mental management of `BAD SUGAR`: anticipation, reward, rescue, grazing, exceptions, bargaining, lapses, resets, and recurring decision labour.

The target is not:

- Food or eating
- Whole fruit
- Every carbohydrate
- Ordinary meal staples
- Molecular purity
- Weight loss
- Diabetes or metabolic treatment
- Eating-disorder treatment
- Every pleasant sweet taste

### Functional personas

1. **Sweet-Reward Reader** — taste, dessert, sweet drinks, reward, or a quick lift appears uniquely valuable.
2. **Restriction Veteran** — denial creates battle; one lapse is interpreted as total failure.
3. **Context Carrier** — emotion, social expectation, convenience, availability, and time pressure make sugar seem inevitable.
4. **Informed Household Translator** — labels and household decisions matter, but knowledge risks becoming purity surveillance or caregiver moralizing.
5. **Managed-Truce Maintainer** — boundaries and resets have brought genuine relief but still require continuing labour.
6. **Decision-Tax Escapee** — the desired outcome is irrelevance, not prohibition or a perfect streak.

The household persona incorporates research personas P-04 and P-05. Caregiver behavior never becomes evidence of the caregiver’s own abstinence or freedom.

### Load-bearing false belief

**BAD SUGAR gives me a special pleasure, reward, comfort, or quick lift that ordinary food and ordinary life cannot provide, so removing it would leave me deprived.**

The book disputes uniqueness, ownership, necessity, and irreplaceability. It does not deny pleasant taste, calories, appetite, distress, celebration, or the reader’s reported experience.

### Redefinition

**GOOD SUGAR:** sugars and starches occurring in ordinary nourishment, including whole fruit, vegetables, legumes, nuts, plain dairy, oats, rice, potatoes, bread, pasta, and other ordinary meal staples.

**BAD SUGAR:** either:

1. A sugar-sweetened drink, confection, dessert, sweet baked good, or added-sugar sweet snack, whether sweetened with sugar, honey, or syrup; or
2. A genuinely ambiguous refined savoury snack functioning as a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or automatic nibble rather than ordinary meal food.

Behavioral-role inquiry applies only to the genuinely gray refined-savoury edge. It cannot exempt a core sweet drink, confection, dessert, sweet baked good, or sweet snack.

Small incidental ingredients in an ordinary meal do not make the meal BAD SUGAR.

**Definitional decree:** **Throughout this book, whenever I say sugar, a sugar hit, or quitting sugar, take me to mean BAD SUGAR as defined here—not fruit, ordinary meals, or every carbohydrate.**

### Totality inside the line

The destination is no planned BAD SUGAR doses. This is practical total freedom, not molecular purity or loss of autonomy. Moderation remains a legal and moral option, but recurring exceptions, bargaining, and resets are not renamed desire-level freedom.

### Molecule Margin

`the Molecule Margin` covers:

- Trace ingredients
- Uncertain restaurant sauces
- An accidental bite
- An honest classification mistake
- Incidental ingredients in an ordinary meal

It prevents panic and all-or-nothing collapse. It never converts a planned exception into an accident.

### Conditional exclusions

One hundred percent juice, dried fruit, non-nutritive sweeteners, alcohol, caffeine, and adjacent indulgences are outside the required quit. Optional later inquiry does not alter the BAD SUGAR line or the success criterion.

### Fork decisions

| Fork | Locked position |
|---|---|
| Fork 1 — personification | Externalize the sugar trap, the learned sweet-reward script, the deprivation method, and the documented Demand Machine. Never invent an inner creature, parasite, attacking voice, or neurological entity. |
| Fork 2 — autonomy and totality | Preserve the reader’s authority while making no planned BAD SUGAR doses the simpler destination. Never command abstinence or claim moderation is impossible. |

| Fork 4 — villain | Direct justified anger toward documented actor-specific formulation, testing, packaging, promotion, and marketing, and toward the deprivation method. Do not attribute intent to generic retailers, workplaces, cafés, celebrations, families, or clinicians. |

### Clinical perimeter

Medical glucose, prescribed nutrition, diabetes or metabolic treatment, pregnancy-related dietary advice, persistent weakness, and eating-disorder care sit outside the belief method.

Qualified clinical instructions outrank the book. The book supplies no medication changes, glucose targets, calorie targets, carbohydrate prescription, withdrawal schedule, or crisis treatment.

Every frozen instruction capable of conflicting with care contains its own clinical exception. A detached instruction must remain safe without relying on another page.

## 2. Through-line and recurring devices

The reader arrives wanting the sugar debate to stop but expecting that stopping means losing a pleasure. The book first protects ordinary food, clinical care, autonomy, and accidental molecules. It then shows that a choice can be genuine while its apparent value has been learned: a rehearsed reward proposition can activate anticipation, and eating may quiet the episode while receiving more credit than its taste earned. Benefit chapters return accomplishment, comfort, convenience, belonging, care, and celebration to their actual sources. Documentary evidence distinguishes actor-specific commercial optimization from ordinary availability, while graded science distinguishes felt difficulty from unsupported addiction, withdrawal, and compulsion claims. The book then exposes deprivation, powerless identity, purity, permanent rule systems, planned exceptions, and fear as ways of keeping BAD SUGAR important. One bounded testimony illustrates the difference between suppression and desire-level quiet without claiming efficacy. The final movement audits the installed understanding, settles calendar delay once, offers a reader-owned threshold, pre-interprets lapses and social exposure, retires the teaching tools, and leaves ordinary life with no sugar question left to settle.

Recurring devices:

- `BAD SUGAR`
- The daily-decision bill
- Pleasant taste versus excessive credit
- The dessert pager
- The sugar trap
- The sweet-reward script and open loop
- The deprivation method and sugar standoff
- The Demand Machine
- Claim grades: `SUPPORTED`, `MIXED`, `CONTESTED`
- The temporary Ordinary Eating Compass
- Suppression, managed truce, early signals, and one self-report of desire-level freedom
- The cumulative instruction spine
- The terminal response to a sugar thought

No recurring device may become biomedical proof, proof of universal commercial coordination, or a lifelong tracking practice.
