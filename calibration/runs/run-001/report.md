## Master-plan failure autopsy

The plans grew from 16,405 to 17,389 to 19,166 words. Every reviewer agreed that the 24-chapter arc, all eight engine functions, named structural slots, and exact 62,000-word budget were present. The recurring failures were contradictions among duplicated representations: mantra sheet versus audit versus chapter spec versus SUMMARY; repeated evidence payloads; occurrence arithmetic; cumulative continuity state; and copied anatomy/analogy matrices. Adding detail moved the contradiction rather than removing it. The mechanism is **denormalized deterministic bookkeeping**, not inadequate model reasoning or missing book architecture. Three planning calls used 91,499, 132,927, and 127,817 native tokens at `ultra`; more reasoning did not repair a contract that asked the model to maintain several redundant sources of truth.

## Diagnosis (ranked, each gap mapped to its owning generic asset)
1. The planning contract makes the model maintain the same decision in several exact forms. Local consistency defects dominate review even when the whole-book architecture is coherent. Owners: `prompts/master-plan-skill-v2.md`, `prompts/master-plan-reviewer-v2.md`, and the plan template.
2. The reviewer judges prewritten chapter anatomy and occurrence bookkeeping before any prose exists, instead of judging whether the plan gives a strong writer a coherent job, evidence, guardrails, and arc. Owner: `prompts/master-plan-reviewer-v2.md`.
3. Evidence must be available to isolated writers, but copying full research into multiple chapter representations creates new traceability failure surfaces. Owner: the master-plan prompt's evidence handoff contract.
