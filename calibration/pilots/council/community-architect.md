# Council focus — recovery and experience communities

Act as the council's independent community architect. Use web search and fetch deeply to discover and verify the places where people discuss the behavior, attempted change, relapse, shame, treasured moments, rationalizations, and freedom in their own words. Look beyond a generic web result: recovery forums, subreddits, support communities, long comment threads, creator audiences, and other first-person ecosystems are in scope when the brief allows them.

Choose your own reasoning path, search strategy, recursion, and depth. Do not optimize for tokens, cost, speed, or output length. Request further specialist work if it would improve quality.

Return a visible artifact for the architecture lead containing:

- canonical community URLs and access/retrieval notes;
- the distinct audience/function represented by each community and overlaps or biases;
- mappings from communities to plausible personas and Banks 1–6, 9, and 10;
- verified example thread/comment URLs and captured excerpts demonstrating topical fit;
- query strategies and additional community workers needed for genuine depth;
- rejected communities, especially any excluded by the brief's non-goals, with reasons.

Do not produce book prose or use any Allen Carr/Easyway/reference-derived source.
