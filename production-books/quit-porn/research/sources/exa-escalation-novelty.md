## Sub-question 8: Escalation to extreme content — novelty-seeking, tolerance, sensitization mechanisms

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9259837/
  DATE: 2022
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: 27 high-PIPU tendency participants vs 25 low-PIPU tendency; EEG/ERP oddball paradigm
  KEY_CLAIM: Individuals with high tendencies toward problematic internet pornography use show larger late positive potential (LPP) ERP responses (500–800ms) to NOVEL vs familiar sexual images — the first electrophysiological evidence that PIPU brains allocate MORE attentional resources to novel sexual stimuli, directly mapping to novelty-seeking as a driver of escalation.
  MECHANISM: Mesocorticolimbic dopaminergic systems are activated by both novelty and drugs of abuse; exposure to novel stimuli increases dopamine in the nucleus accumbens, the same mechanism as drug reward. Enhanced novelty processing facilitates ongoing pornography consumption and maintains PIPU.
  STRENGTH: EEG is an objective neurophysiological measure (not self-report); replicates behavioral finding of Banca et al. (2016) CSB novelty preference with a brain measure.
  STANCE: supported
  GAP: Small sample (N=52); cross-sectional; PIPU "tendency" rather than clinical diagnosis; cannot determine whether enhanced novelty processing precedes or results from heavy use.

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4683093/
  DATE: 2016
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: 22 CSB males vs 40 controls; behavioral + fMRI (Cambridge University, Voon/Banca et al.)
  KEY_CLAIM: CSB is associated with enhanced novelty preference for sexual images AND generalized conditioning enhancement; dACC habituation to repeated sexual images mediates novelty preference; this neurobiological profile provides the mechanism for why users require ever-newer content to maintain arousal.
  MECHANISM: Normal viewers habituate to repeated sexual content (arousal falls); CSB individuals habituate faster in the dACC, driving stronger preference for novelty to restore stimulation. Simultaneously, cue-conditioning to sexual rewards is generalized beyond sexual stimuli in CSB — amplifying approach behaviors toward any novelty.
  STRENGTH: Controlled fMRI study; establishes brain mechanism for escalation rather than just behavioral observation.
  STANCE: supported
  GAP: Male CSB sample only; all participants had pre-existing CSB diagnosis — cannot demonstrate this mechanism precedes heavy use.

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6426391/
  DATE: 2019
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: College students from China (N not specified in extract); moderated mediation model
  KEY_CLAIM: Sexual sensation seeking (SSS) — tendency to pursue novel, intense sexual experiences — predicts problematic internet pornography use (PIPU) via increased engagement in online sexual activities; this pathway is moderated by the third-person effect (perceiving porn has greater impact on others than oneself) and is primarily applicable to males.
  MECHANISM: Individuals with high SSS are attracted to the Internet for its novelty, anonymity, and easy access; they engage in more online sexual activities (OSAs), which reinforce conditioned behavioral patterns; the escalating novelty demands of SSS drive PIPU. Males show stronger positive-reinforcement motivation pathways than females.
  STRENGTH: Theory-grounded model (I-PACE); moderated mediation tests mechanism vs mere correlation; gender-stratified analysis.
  STANCE: supported
  GAP: Chinese university sample (cross-sectional); moderated mediation requires strong causal assumptions; SSS measure may overlap with PIPU.

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31130200/
  DATE: 2019
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: 248 male adolescents (mean age 16.1); PROBIOPS study; 24-month 5-wave longitudinal
  KEY_CLAIM: The Content Progression Thesis (CPT) — that pornography use leads to viewing increasingly more extreme violent/coercive content through satiation — is NOT supported longitudinally in adolescent males; preference for violent/coercive content DECREASED over 24 months and was unrelated to pornography use trajectories.
  MECHANISM: Satiation-based conditioning escalation to violence/coercion does not occur as a universal, automatic developmental process in adolescent porn users over 2 years.
  STRENGTH: First longitudinal test of CPT; rigorous dual-domain growth curve modeling; pre-registered.
  STANCE: contested (challenges CPT specifically)
  GAP: Only tests escalation to violence/coercion — not fetish content, age of models, or other escalation dimensions; male adolescents only; does not test adult compulsive users where escalation may be more pronounced.

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2026.1815849/full
  DATE: 2026-04-21
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: 200 UK adults with minor-onset pornography use (first access before 18)
  KEY_CLAIM: Qualitative research with UK men investigated for accessing child sexual abuse material (ICSAM) found that some describe becoming "bored" with general adult pornography and escalating to more extreme content due to easy access; 41.5% of participants in this study primarily consumed violent or degrading pornography; 45% of Pornhub scenes and 35% of XVideos scenes contain at least one act of physical aggression.
  MECHANISM: Positive reinforcement loop theory + neuroadaptation: repeated exposure to rewarding stimuli produces dopamine surges reinforcing behavior; neuroadaptation reduces natural dopamine responsiveness, contributing to tolerance and escalating consumption. 3AM Theory (Acquisition, Activation, Application) explains how pornographic scripts are internalized and activated by cues.
  STRENGTH: 2026 data; cites empirical content analysis of major platforms (Fritz et al.); directly references clinical case accounts of escalation.
  VERBATIM: "Qualitative research with men investigated for accessing IIOCs in the UK suggests that some individuals describe becoming 'bored' of general adult pornography and escalating to more extreme content, often due to easy access on adult pornography websites"
  STANCE: mixed
  GAP: Content analysis data (Fritz et al.) is platform-specific and may not reflect all pornography; the qualitative escalation data comes from a high-severity clinical sample (men investigated for ICSAM) and cannot be generalized to typical pornography users.
