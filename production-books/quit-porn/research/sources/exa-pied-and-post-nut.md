## Sub-question 6: PIED (porn-induced erectile dysfunction) — empirical studies and mechanism detail

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1743609521005713
  DATE: 2022
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: 942 heterosexual men aged 18–44; cross-sectional online survey from Reddit pornography subgroups
  KEY_CLAIM: Pornography use frequency and duration alone are NOT associated with erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation, or sexual satisfaction; self-perceived IP addiction IS associated with — and uniquely predicts — increased ED, premature ejaculation, and individual sexual dissatisfaction.
  MECHANISM: The distinction is between objective use (frequency/duration) and subjective perceived addiction; the latter, which captures the experience of compulsive, life-interfering, distressing use, is the operative variable. "Simply watching a lot of porn" or "watching for long durations" was not sufficient to produce sexual dysfunction.
  STRENGTH: N=942; distinguishes frequency vs duration vs perceived addiction; controls for depression, anxiety, stress, comorbid sexual dysfunction.
  STANCE: mixed
  GAP: Cross-sectional; self-report; Reddit sample (men already engaged with pornography communities); does not capture casual/non-community users.

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30621919/
  DATE: 2019
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Three samples of sexually active male porn users: undergraduates (N=147); US-representative online (N=297); 4-wave longitudinal (N=433 baseline; Mechanical Turk) — Grubbs & Gola
  KEY_CLAIM: Across all three samples: no consistent association between mere pornography use and erectile dysfunction; cross-sectional association between self-reported problematic use and ED exists, but latent growth curve analyses show no significant causal relationship over time — problematic use and ED co-occur but do not predict each other's trajectories.
  MECHANISM: The association between perceived problematic use and ED likely reflects shared underlying factors (anxiety, relationship distress, psychological distress) rather than pornography causing ED physiologically.
  STRENGTH: Three samples; cross-sectional and longitudinal methods; IIEF-5 validated measure of erectile function.
  STANCE: contested (challenges causal PIED narrative directly)
  GAP: Self-report; no medical covariates (no vascular or hormonal measurement); does not assess classical conditioning pathway specifically.

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: peer_reviewed
  URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11934-024-01213-9
  DATE: 2024-06-26
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Pediatric urology review; adolescent males; clinical practice report noting 31x increase in adolescent males presenting with ED since 2014
  KEY_CLAIM: There has been a 31-fold increase in adolescent males presenting with erectile dysfunction in one large pediatric urology practice since 2014; majority of cases are psychogenic (anxiety/depression); pornography consumption is identified as a contributing factor warranting screening; treatment includes reassurance, CBT, and low-dose tadalafil rehabilitation.
  MECHANISM: Psychogenic pathway: stress from anxiety and depression raises cortisol, increases vascular resistance, and produces ED. Pornography screens for separate contribution via validated instruments (CYPAT + IIEF-5 from Jacobs et al. 2021, which found increased pornography use increases ED rates).
  STRENGTH: Real-world clinical practice data; 2024 review; identifies practical treatment pathway.
  STANCE: supported (for psychogenic + pornography screening as clinical standard)
  GAP: Clinical observation from one practice; no controlled study; causality from pornography to clinical ED not isolated.

## Sub-question 7: Post-nut clarity / post-orgasm phenomenology — mechanism and lived experience

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: news
  URL: https://mindlabneuroscience.com/brain-chemicals-during-sex/
  DATE: 2023-09-27
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Clinical neuroscience synthesis; couples therapy application
  KEY_CLAIM: Orgasm produces a simultaneous release of dopamine, oxytocin, endorphins, and vasopressin with temporary deactivation of the lateral orbitofrontal cortex (self-monitoring/behavioral control); post-orgasm, prolactin surges in men (producing drowsiness/withdrawal) while oxytocin remains elevated longer in women — creating a predictable neurochemical mismatch.
  MECHANISM: Dopamine drives anticipation (mesolimbic pathway); orgasm = simultaneous co-release of dopamine + oxytocin + endorphins + vasopressin; lateral orbitofrontal cortex deactivates (ego dissolution, no self-monitoring); serotonin dominates resolution phase. A post-orgasmic consolidation window (15–45 min) requires sustained skin-to-skin contact for secondary oxytocin release; without it, bonding potential is lost. Prolactin surge in men temporarily dampens reward system, producing "flatness or mild dysphoria."
  STRENGTH: Synthesis citing Georgiadis & Kringelbach (2012) neuroimaging; Uvnas Moberg oxytocin research; clinically applied in couples therapy context.
  VERBATIM: "Prolactin, which surges post-orgasm in both sexes, also temporarily dampens the reward system, producing flatness or mild dysphoria. The feeling reflects a gap between neurochemical arousal and relational closeness, not a verdict on the relationship."
  STANCE: mixed
  GAP: Not a primary research article; some claims (orbitofrontal cortex deactivation) cite specific neuroimaging work but the synthesis has not been independently peer-reviewed.

---

- SOURCE_TYPE: news
  URL: https://www.aol.com/articles/sad-sex-post-coital-dysphoria-141000942.html
  DATE: 2025-09-22
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Consumer health article; clinical commentary by Dr. Justin Houman, urologist, Cedars-Sinai
  KEY_CLAIM: Post-coital dysphoria (PCD) affects up to 41% of men at some point in their lifetime; it is characterized by inexplicable sadness, irritability, or emptiness after orgasm even when sex was good; both post-nut clarity and PCD are "two ends of the same physiological spectrum."
  MECHANISM: Dopamine surges during sexual arousal then drops abruptly post-orgasm; prolactin surge (relaxing in some men, irritability-inducing in others depending on brain chemistry); oxytocin, serotonin, and cortisol all shift post-orgasm. Low testosterone amplifies the crash. Partnered sex within a committed relationship may buffer PCD via oxytocin release; unresolved relational conflict can worsen it.
  STRENGTH: Clinical commentary from practicing urologist; cites specific prevalence data (41% lifetime in men).
  VERBATIM: "dopamine and testosterone aren't the only brain chemicals at play when orgasms trigger bad moods. Oxytocin, serotonin, and cortisol all shift post-O, as well as prolactin, a hormone that can cause feelings of relaxation in some men but feelings of irritability and dysphoria in others"
  STANCE: supported
  GAP: Not a primary study; 41% figure's source not linked directly in excerpt; individual variation in response is high.

  URL: https://celibacytracker.com/blog/nofap-benefits-timeline-day-by-day
  DATE: 2026-03-18
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Aggregated NoFap community experience; day-by-day recovery synthesis
  KEY_CLAIM: The "post-nut doom" (dopamine crash after pornography use) is explained as dopamine D2 receptor downregulation from chronic supranormal stimulation; the flatline period (weeks 2–8 of abstinence) is active receptor recovery; by day 14+ mental haze clears as D2 receptor density normalizes; reboot requires 60–90 days for moderate users, longer for heavy/long-term users.
  MECHANISM: Pornography = supranormal stimulus triggering dopamine beyond natural reward ceiling; brain compensates by reducing D2 receptor density (desensitization); removing the stimulus causes below-baseline dopamine → "post-nut doom" → flatline; recovery = D2 receptor upregulation and prefrontal cortex activity returning.
  STRENGTH: Rich phenomenological synthesis of large community experience; mechanistically consistent with the peer-reviewed animal models above.
  VERBATIM: "Around day 14... the low-grade mental haze you've been living with for years quietly disappears... This is dopamine D2 receptor upregulation: your brain rebuilding receptors that got down-regulated by chronic overstimulation. Natural rewards (a good meal, a hard workout, a real conversation) start producing pleasure again."
  STANCE: contested (mechanistic claims are community consensus extrapolating from neuroscience; not clinically validated timelines)
  GAP: Not a scientific source; timelines are anecdotal; D2 receptor upregulation claim is plausible but not directly measured in abstaining humans in this context.

  URL: https://www.einseo.com/blogs/blog/the-emotional-landscape-of-post-nut-clarity-understanding-what-comes-after-orgasmic
  DATE: 2026-05-08
  POPULATION/CONTEXT: Consumer wellness synthesis; post-nut clarity phenomenology
  KEY_CLAIM: "Post-nut clarity" is a real and complex emotional experience — characterized by sudden mental shift, emotional distance, or hyper-awareness after orgasm; post-orgasm shame in men commonly produces a "crash" of guilt and self-judgment when the behavior conflicts with internalized values.

  STANCE: contested
  GAP: Not a research source; phenomenological synthesis. The "collision between desire and moral judgment" insight aligns with Grubbs' moral incongruence model but is not empirically derived here.
