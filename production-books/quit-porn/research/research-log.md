## Step 1: Sub-question Decomposition (2026-06-07)

Sub-questions decomposed (5–8 required):

1. **Neuroscience/dopamine mechanisms** — exact dopamine desensitization, DeltaFosB accumulation, prefrontal cortex downregulation, withdrawal neurochemistry. Dump cites Kühn & Gallinat, Voon et al, I-PACE but has summaries only, no actual text from the studies.
2. **Contested-claims landscape** — Grubbs moral-incongruence model vs addiction model: dump covers both but lacks recent rebuttals and Grubbs' own latest data (2023–2025). Also missing: Prause's follow-up work, recent meta-analyses on whether CSBD meets addiction criteria.
3. **What specifically helps people quit** — CBT, ACT, mindfulness evidence: dump has a table with Crosby & Twohig 93% reduction, but lacks actual RCT text, effect sizes, comparison data, and newer (2024–2025) intervention studies.
4. **Adolescent/developmental effects** — dump barely covers teen exposure, developmental impact on sexual templates, age-at-first-exposure effects. This is a major gap.
5. **Partner/relationship impacts** — dump has betrayal trauma from r/loveafterporn but lacks peer-reviewed data on relationship satisfaction, partner PTSD, couples therapy outcomes.
6. **PIED (porn-induced erectile dysfunction)** — dump covers the debate but lacks recent empirical studies and mechanism detail (conditioning, classical vs operant, reboot timelines).
7. **Post-nut clarity / post-orgasm phenomenology** — entirely absent from dump. The neurochemical basis (prolactin, oxytocin, serotonin crash) and why this creates the guilt-shame-relapse cycle.
8. **Escalation to extreme content** — mechanism of tolerance/escalation (novelty-seeking, sensitization) and what content categories users migrate to. Dump alludes to it but no studies.
