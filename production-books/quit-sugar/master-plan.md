# Master Plan — Quit Sugar

## Authority and source boundary

This is the single build brief for a full-length, 21-chapter book. Its only factual inputs are the accepted lived-experience and scientific-evidence syntheses named in the evidence ledger below. The plan does not authorize an invented author story, invented clinical outcome, universal mechanism, or claim that this method has been tested. No accepted source supplies an author-origin story or a method-success pedigree, so trust must come from accurate reader recognition, transparent limits, and one bounded lived possibility—not fabricated authority.

Every chapter uses the full-length anatomy: IN THIS CHAPTER, one italic thesis, one belief move through titled sections, an assigned instruction at the climax when applicable, an ALL-CAPS landing only where earned, and SUMMARY. Previews and summaries are recap zones; outside them, only frozen mantras repeat verbatim. CH-21 is the §B10 exception: a page-skip gate followed by the numbered instruction recap and its chapter cross-references.

## Redefinition, totality, margin, and safety

### RD-01 — BAD SUGAR

**GOOD SUGAR** means sugars and starches in ordinary nourishment: whole fruit, vegetables, legumes, nuts, plain dairy, and ordinary meal staples such as oats, rice, potatoes, bread, and pasta. Their molecules do not place them inside the target.

**BAD SUGAR** means:

1. The categorical core: a sugar-sweetened drink, confection, dessert, sweet baked good, or added-sugar sweet snack, including products sweetened with sugar, honey, or syrup.
2. The gray savoury edge: a genuinely ambiguous refined savoury snack when it functions as a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or automatic nibble rather than ordinary meal food.

Core items stay inside the line regardless of hunger, celebration, convenience, homemade status, or natural, organic, dark, low-sugar, or healthier branding. Behavioral-role inquiry applies only at the genuinely gray savoury edge and can never exempt a core item. Small incidental ingredients in an ordinary meal do not convert that meal into BAD SUGAR.

**Definitional decree:** Throughout the book, sugar, a sugar hit, and quitting sugar mean BAD SUGAR—not fruit, ordinary meals, or every carbohydrate.

### RD-02 — Totality inside the line

The destination is no planned BAD SUGAR doses. This is practical total cessation, not molecular purity. The reader retains moral agency, but the book commands one stable exit because a planned exception preserves the proposition that BAD SUGAR owns a benefit worth scheduling. The moderation pincer is: if BAD SUGAR provides no unique benefit, there is no reason to preserve it for occasional use; if it does provide one, rationing makes it more precious and keeps the debate alive.

### RD-03 — The Molecule Margin

The Molecule Margin covers a trace ingredient, uncertain restaurant sauce, accidental bite, or honest classification mistake. The reader notices it without panic, learns if useful, and continues from the decision already made. It never converts a planned exception into an accident. A slip does not revive the trap unless the belief is invited back; “I got away with it” is not permission to plan another dose.

### RD-04 — Protected clinical perimeter

Medical glucose, prescribed nutrition, diabetes or metabolic treatment, pregnancy-related dietary advice, and eating-disorder care are outside the method. Qualified clinical instructions outrank this book. The book never diagnoses, prescribes, counters a clinician, recommends restriction to a reader with an eating disorder, or uses an epistemic firewall against qualified care. Persistent weakness, distress around food, or signs of disordered eating route outward to appropriate professional care.

### RD-05 — Adjacent categories

One hundred percent juice, dried fruit, non-nutritive sweeteners, alcohol, caffeine, and other adjacent indulgences are not required quits and do not define success. Whole fruit and ordinary meal carbohydrates are protected from category creep. No adjacent “bonus” becomes a hidden condition of freedom.

### Mechanism-character register

### Trap register

Use: BAD SUGAR, planned dose, sugar hit, feed, bait, con, sales act, borrowed credit, sugar trap, Sugar Showman, Sugar Flea, deprivation method, sugar standoff, Demand Machine, decision tax, default lane. “Dose” and “hit” are rhetorical trap labels, never clinical diagnoses. Use “brainwashing” for installed belief only when the prose immediately identifies learning, advertising, or repeated cultural rehearsal; never imply mind control.

### Freedom register

Use: escape, free, freedom, relief, wonderful news, cheerful certainty, finished, ordinary eating, ordinary pleasure, quiet decision, walk on, rejoice, celebrate, change nothing else, happy to be free. Freedom language is seeded early, restrained through the middle demolition, and dominates CH-18 through CH-21.

### Banned willpower register

Do not use as the book's own solution: give up sugar, sacrifice, resist, stay strong, discipline, white-knuckle, abstinence journey, recovery journey, one day at a time, clean streak, cheat, guilty pleasure, good eater, bad eater, food sin, detox, purity, substitute treat, trigger avoidance, earn food. These terms may appear only inside a reader objection or in an explicit demolition of the deprivation method. “Quit” remains available as a plain verb; “giving up” remains available only when rejecting the loss frame.

### Reader dialect

Use the persona lines in PR-01–PR-06 and the menu in J-01–J-22. Preserve bounded packet phrases when directly quoted: “cash wrap food” is one person's term; “carb window” is one participant's term; “eat our emotions,” “salt person,” and “sugar craving” are limited community phrasing. Never present any as universal slang or a clinical category.

### Temporary Ordinary Eating Compass

The Compass is not a mantra, identity, dashboard, or lifelong authority. It contains:

- **LX-C01 — meal-or-hit check:** only for a genuinely gray refined-savoury snack; decide whether it is serving ordinary eating or a discretionary reward, rescue, graze, or nibble. It cannot exempt a categorical core item.
- **LX-C02 — pleasure-source audit:** a temporary close look at a selected cherished scene to return credit to taste, people, rest, ritual, effort, hunger, and occasion.
- **LX-C03 — Molecule Margin:** the RD-03 interpretation for accidents and honest ambiguity.

LX-C01 and LX-C02 retire when the distinction becomes obvious. LX-C03 remains a quiet interpretive margin, not a reason to inspect every ingredient.
